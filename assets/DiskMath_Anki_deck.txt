What does a | b mean, and name 3 basic properties	a divides b: ∃k∈ℤ with b=ak.<br>• a|b ∧ a|c ⇒ a|(b+c)<br>• a|b ⇒ a|bc<br>• a|b ∧ b|c ⇒ a|c	NumberTheory
Division with remainder (Euclidean division) statement	For a∈ℤ, b>0: ∃! q,r with a=qb+r and 0≤r<b. q=⌊a/b⌋, r=a mod b.	NumberTheory
Definition of gcd(a,b) via the Euclidean algorithm	gcd(a,b)=gcd(b, a mod b), gcd(a,0)=a. Repeat until remainder 0; last nonzero remainder is the gcd.	NumberTheory
Bézout's identity	gcd(a,b)=d ⇒ ∃ x,y∈ℤ with ax+by=d. Found via the extended Euclidean algorithm.	NumberTheory
How do you find a modular inverse a⁻¹ mod n?	Exists iff gcd(a,n)=1. Use extended Euclid to get ax+ny=1 ⇒ x≡a⁻¹ (mod n).	NumberTheory
Fundamental theorem of arithmetic	Every n>1 has a unique prime factorization (up to order).	NumberTheory
Definition: a ≡ b (mod n)	n | (a−b). Equivalence relation; partitions ℤ into n residue classes.	NumberTheory
Congruence arithmetic rules (add/mult/power)	If a≡b, c≡d (mod n): a+c≡b+d, ac≡bd, aᵏ≡bᵏ (mod n). (Division needs gcd condition.)	NumberTheory
When can you cancel: ac ≡ bc (mod n) ⇒ a ≡ b (mod ?)	a ≡ b (mod n/gcd(c,n)). If gcd(c,n)=1 then a≡b (mod n).	NumberTheory
Solvability of ax ≡ b (mod n)	Solvable iff gcd(a,n) | b. Then exactly gcd(a,n) solutions mod n.	NumberTheory
Chinese Remainder Theorem (statement)	If n₁,…,nₖ pairwise coprime, the system x≡aᵢ (mod nᵢ) has a unique solution mod N=∏nᵢ.	NumberTheory
CRT solution construction	x = Σ aᵢ Nᵢ Mᵢ (mod N), where Nᵢ=N/nᵢ and Mᵢ=Nᵢ⁻¹ (mod nᵢ).	NumberTheory
Fermat's little theorem	p prime, gcd(a,p)=1 ⇒ a^(p−1) ≡ 1 (mod p). Also aᵖ≡a (mod p) for all a.	NumberTheory
Euler's theorem	gcd(a,n)=1 ⇒ a^φ(n) ≡ 1 (mod n).	NumberTheory
Euler's totient φ(n): formula	φ(n)=n·∏_{p|n}(1−1/p). φ(p)=p−1; φ(pᵏ)=pᵏ−pᵏ⁻¹; multiplicative for coprime args.	NumberTheory
RSA key generation	Pick primes p,q; n=pq; φ(n)=(p−1)(q−1); choose e with gcd(e,φ)=1; d≡e⁻¹ (mod φ(n)). Public (e,n), private d.	NumberTheory
RSA encrypt / decrypt (the two formulas)	Encrypt: c ≡ mᵉ (mod n). Decrypt: m ≡ cᵈ (mod n). Works because ed≡1 (mod φ(n)).	NumberTheory
Why RSA decryption works	m^(ed) = m^(1+kφ(n)) ≡ m·(m^φ(n))ᵏ ≡ m (mod n) by Euler's theorem.	NumberTheory
Fast modular exponentiation idea	Square-and-multiply: write exponent in binary, repeatedly square and multiply mod n. O(log e) multiplications.	NumberTheory
Definition of a group (G,∗)	Closure, associativity, identity e, inverses. Abelian if also commutative.	Algebra
Order of a group / order of an element	|G|=#elements. ord(a)=smallest k>0 with aᵏ=e (=∞ if none).	Algebra
Lagrange's theorem	For finite G and subgroup H: |H| divides |G|. Hence ord(a) | |G|.	Algebra
Corollary of Lagrange for element orders	a^|G| = e for all a∈G. (Generalizes Fermat/Euler.)	Algebra
Definition of a cyclic group	G=⟨g⟩={gᵏ}: generated by one element g. Every cyclic group of order n ≅ ℤₙ.	Algebra
Subgroup test (one-step)	Nonempty H⊆G is a subgroup iff ∀a,b∈H: a∗b⁻¹∈H.	Algebra
Definition of a group homomorphism	φ:G→H with φ(ab)=φ(a)φ(b). Then φ(e_G)=e_H and φ(a⁻¹)=φ(a)⁻¹.	Algebra
Kernel and image of a homomorphism	ker φ={g:φ(g)=e_H} (a subgroup of G); im φ a subgroup of H. φ injective ⇔ ker φ={e}.	Algebra
Definition of a ring	(R,+,·): abelian group under +, associative ·, distributive. Commutative/with-unity variants.	Algebra
Definition of a field	Commutative ring with 1≠0 where every nonzero element has a multiplicative inverse. ℤₚ is a field iff p prime.	Algebra
When is ℤₙ a field / when are units?	ℤₙ field ⇔ n prime. Units of ℤₙ = {a : gcd(a,n)=1}, count = φ(n).	Algebra
Number of elementary counting forms (and which)	4: ordered/unordered × with/without repetition (variations & combinations).	Combinatorics
Permutations of n objects; k-permutations	n! total; k-permutations (ordered, no repeat) = n!/(n−k)!.	Combinatorics
Combinations C(n,k) formula	C(n,k)=n!/(k!(n−k)!) = number of k-subsets of an n-set.	Combinatorics
Combinations with repetition (multiset)	Choosing k from n types with repetition = C(n+k−1, k) (stars and bars).	Combinatorics
Pigeonhole principle (general form)	n objects in k boxes ⇒ some box has ≥ ⌈n/k⌉ objects.	Combinatorics
Binomial theorem	(x+y)ⁿ = Σ_{k=0}^{n} C(n,k) xᵏ y^(n−k).	Combinatorics
Pascal's rule	C(n,k)=C(n−1,k−1)+C(n−1,k).	Combinatorics
Inclusion–exclusion (two & general)	|A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B|. General: |∪Aᵢ|=Σ|Aᵢ|−Σ|Aᵢ∩Aⱼ|+… (−1)^(k+1) terms.	Combinatorics
Stirling number 2nd kind S(n,k)	# ways to partition an n-set into k nonempty unlabeled blocks. Recurrence S(n,k)=k·S(n−1,k)+S(n−1,k−1).	Combinatorics
Surjections from n-set onto k-set	k!·S(n,k).	Combinatorics
Sum of first n integers / squares	Σi = n(n+1)/2; Σi² = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6.	Combinatorics
Definition of a (simple) graph; degree	G=(V,E). deg(v)=#incident edges. Handshake: Σdeg(v)=2|E| ⇒ #odd-degree vertices is even.	GraphTheory
Definition of a tree + key equivalences	Connected acyclic graph. Equivalent: connected with |E|=|V|−1; unique path between any two vertices.	GraphTheory
Number of edges in a tree / spanning tree	A tree on n vertices has exactly n−1 edges.	GraphTheory
Eulerian path/circuit conditions	Connected graph: Euler circuit ⇔ every vertex even degree; Euler path ⇔ exactly 0 or 2 odd-degree vertices.	GraphTheory
Hamiltonian cycle (definition + difficulty)	Cycle visiting every vertex exactly once. No simple necessary-and-sufficient condition (NP-hard). Sufficient: Dirac deg≥n/2.	GraphTheory
Definition of a planar graph	Can be drawn in the plane with no edge crossings.	GraphTheory
Euler's formula for connected planar graphs	V − E + F = 2 (F includes the outer face).	GraphTheory
Edge bound for simple planar graphs	E ≤ 3V−6 (V≥3); if triangle-free, E ≤ 2V−4. Used to prove non-planarity.	GraphTheory
Kuratowski's theorem	A graph is planar ⇔ it contains no subdivision of K₅ or K₃,₃.	GraphTheory
Chromatic number χ(G); bounds	Min colors for proper vertex coloring. χ ≤ Δ+1 (greedy); bipartite ⇔ χ≤2 ⇔ no odd cycle.	GraphTheory
Four/Five color theorem	Every planar graph is 4-colorable (5-color theorem has an easy proof).	GraphTheory
Bipartite graph characterization	G bipartite ⇔ no odd-length cycle ⇔ 2-colorable.	GraphTheory
Walk vs path vs cycle	Walk: edge sequence (repeats allowed). Path: no repeated vertices. Cycle: closed path.	GraphTheory
Definition of a flow network and a flow	Directed graph, source s, sink t, capacities c(e)≥0. Flow f: 0≤f(e)≤c(e), conservation at all v≠s,t.	Flows
Value of a flow; definition of an s-t cut	|f| = net flow out of s. Cut (S,T): s∈S, t∈T; capacity = Σ capacities of edges S→T.	Flows
Max-flow min-cut theorem	Max flow value = min cut capacity.	Flows
Ford–Fulkerson method	Repeatedly find an augmenting path in the residual graph, push min residual capacity. Terminates with integer capacities.	Flows
Residual capacity / residual graph	c_f(u,v)=c(u,v)−f(u,v); plus backward edges with capacity f(u,v). Augmenting path = s→t path with all c_f>0.	Flows
Integrality theorem for flows	If all capacities are integers, there is a maximum flow with all integer flow values.	Flows
CRC / polynomial coding idea	Treat bitstring as polynomial over GF(2); codeword divisible by generator g(x). Error detected if remainder ≠ 0.	Algebra
The 4 elementary counting forms — # ways to pick k from n (the GUARANTEED Koch MC)	Ordered + repetition: nᵏ.<br>Ordered, no repetition: n^(k̲)=n!/(n−k)! (falling factorial).<br>Unordered, no repetition: C(n,k).<br>Unordered + repetition: C(n+k−1,k).	Combinatorics ExamHotlist
Falling factorial n^(k̲) — meaning	n(n−1)…(n−k+1) = n!/(n−k)! = # ordered selections of k from n without repetition (k-permutations).	Combinatorics ExamHotlist
How many integers in [1,N] are divisible by a, b, or c? (guaranteed MC)	Inclusion–exclusion: ⌊N/a⌋+⌊N/b⌋+⌊N/c⌋ − ⌊N/lcm(a,b)⌋ − ⌊N/lcm(a,c)⌋ − ⌊N/lcm(b,c)⌋ + ⌊N/lcm(a,b,c)⌋.	Combinatorics ExamHotlist
Vandermonde's identity	C(m+n,k) = Σⱼ C(m,j)C(n,k−j). Special case: C(2n,n) = Σ C(n,k)².	Combinatorics
Ramsey number R(3,3)	R(3,3)=6: any 2-coloring of K₆'s edges has a monochromatic triangle (K₅ can avoid it).	Combinatorics
Cayley's formula — # labeled trees / spanning trees of Kₙ	n^(n−2) labeled trees on n vertices = # spanning trees of Kₙ.	GraphTheory ExamHotlist
Number of distinct Hamiltonian cycles in Kₙ	(n−1)!/2.	GraphTheory
Tree degree-sum condition	Tree on n vertices has n−1 edges ⇒ Σ deg = 2(n−1). A sequence is a tree's degrees iff all dᵢ≥1 and Σdᵢ=2(n−1).	GraphTheory ExamHotlist
Why exactly five Platonic solids?	Faces are regular q-gons, p meeting per vertex: 1/p+1/q>1/2 has only 5 integer solutions (tetra/cube/octa/dodeca/icosa).	GraphTheory
Order and structure of the dihedral group Dₙ	|Dₙ|=2n: n rotations + n reflections. Symmetries of a regular n-gon. Non-abelian and non-cyclic for n≥3.	Algebra ExamHotlist
Symmetry group of the regular tetrahedron — order?	Rotations only: 12 (≅ A₄). Including reflections: 24 (≅ S₄).	Algebra ExamHotlist
Order and subgroups of S₃	|S₃|=6 (smallest non-abelian group). Subgroups: {e}, three ⟨transposition⟩ (order 2), ⟨3-cycle⟩=A₃ (order 3), S₃.	Algebra
Group of even order must contain… (pairing argument)	An element of order 2 (self-inverse a=a⁻¹). Proof: pair a with a⁻¹; without a self-inverse, elements pair off ⇒ |G| odd, contradiction.	Algebra ExamHotlist
LCG xₙ₊₁=(a·xₙ+c) mod m — full-period condition (Hull–Dobell)	Period = m iff: gcd(c,m)=1; (a−1) divisible by every prime factor of m; (a−1) divisible by 4 if 4|m.	NumberTheory
CRC encoding procedure (generator g(x), degree r, over GF(2))	Append r zeros: m(x)·xʳ. Divide by g(x) mod 2 → remainder = check bits. Codeword = m(x)·xʳ + remainder (divisible by g). Receiver: remainder≠0 ⇒ error.	Algebra ExamHotlist
Arithmetic in 𝔽₄={0,1,α,α²} with α²=α+1	Char 2 (x+x=0): α+1=α², α+α²=1, 1+α²=α. Mult: α·α=α², α·α²=1, α²·α²=α. Every nonzero element is invertible.	Algebra
Nim — winning strategy	Nim-sum = XOR of pile sizes. Position is losing (for the mover) iff nim-sum=0. Winning move: change a pile to make nim-sum 0.	Algebra
Divisibility rule for 11 (and why)	11 | n iff 11 divides the alternating digit sum. Because 10≡−1 (mod 11), so 10ᵏ≡(−1)ᵏ.	NumberTheory
