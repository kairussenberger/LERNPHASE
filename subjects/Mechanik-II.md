## The core (what Mechanik II actually is)

Rigid-body **dynamics**: kinematics of points & rigid bodies, Newton–Euler equations, work–energy and momentum/impulse theorems, moments of inertia, and vibrations/oscillations. The exam rewards a **fixed mechanical workflow** far more than clever insight: free-body diagram → choose principle (Impuls/Drall/Energie) → set up equations → solve. Speed and cleanliness are graded.

## Priority order

1. **Past exams are the syllabus.** You have ~25 of them — this is the single highest-leverage resource. Drill the recent **Klausuren FS20–FS25** timed first, then Basisprüfungen.
2. **Your own Zwischenprüfung (Mai 2026).** Re-solve it from blank against the `Musterlösung`; the final reuses the same problem types. `Prüfung/Zwischenprüfung 04.05.2026 18_30 Uhr/`.
3. **Serien 1–12** for mechanics you're still shaky on (`Übung/`).
4. **Formelsammlung fluency** — since it's provided, every second spent hunting in it during the exam is lost. Rehearse *where* each formula lives.
